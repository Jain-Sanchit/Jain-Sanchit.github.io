## Why I started with Niyo (and what it taught me)

When I first started travelling abroad in 2024, Niyo Global was the obvious pick. It was the most-recommended forex card in every YouTube video, Reddit thread, and personal finance blog I read. I used it in **Thailand, Malaysia, and Singapore in 2024** and spent roughly **₹17,000 across two trips**.

What I genuinely liked about Niyo:

- **0% forex markup** — same as Scapia, this is the table-stakes feature
- **Real-time conversion** with no nasty surprises on the statement
- **Visa acceptance** — worked everywhere I tried in Southeast Asia
- **App was clean** — easy to load money, easy to track spend

But there was friction that grew on me over time.

### The Niyo friction nobody talks about

**1. You have to open a bank account.** In 2024, using Niyo Global meant opening an **SBM Bank** account on the Niyo app. That's a separate KYC flow with another bank.

**2. You have to pre-deposit every rupee you'll spend.** Niyo is fundamentally a prepaid product. If I wanted to spend ₹40,000 on a trip, I had to deposit ₹40,000 first. This locks up cash, and any unspent balance just sits there until your next trip — or you withdraw it back.

**3. There's no upside beyond zero forex.** Niyo doesn't reward you for spending. You get the FX rate, and that's it. For someone like me who was already spending ₹95k+ per trip, leaving 2% rewards on the table started to feel expensive.

> **A note on Niyo's current product in 2026:** Niyo has evolved since I used it. The exact onboarding flow, partner bank, and variants may have changed. Before applying, check niyo.com for their latest product details — my notes above describe my 2024 experience.

## Why I switched to Scapia

I got the **Scapia Federal Credit Card** in late 2025 and used it on my **Japan trip in November 2025**. Then **Indonesia and Malaysia in February 2026**. Then the **UK in June 2026**. I am planning to use it again on Singapore and Europe trips later this year.

The thing that flipped me was simple: **Scapia gave me a ₹5,00,000 credit limit without asking for any deposit.** That alone is a big deal — I will explain why in the iPhone story below — but Scapia stacks several more wins on top of zero forex.

### What I actually like about Scapia (in order of how often I use it)

#### 1. It's a credit card, not a prepaid card

This sounds like a small distinction but it changes everything. With Niyo, my "limit" was whatever I had deposited. With Scapia, my limit is **₹5,00,000** — I don't have to plan trip budgets in advance or scramble to top up.

#### 2. Real rewards on every spend (including UPI)

Scapia gives me **10 Scapia Coins for every ₹100 spent**. Each coin is worth roughly ₹0.20, so the effective rate is **around 2% back** — and crucially, this applies on **UPI transactions too**.

I have already redeemed coins for a **DailyObjects bag** through the Scapia app's reward store. It arrived in two days. The bag cost me zero rupees out of pocket.

#### 3. Airport privileges that actually trigger

This is the underrated part of Scapia. Spend **₹20,000 in a month**, and the next month you get:

- **Unlimited domestic lounge access**
- **Free meals at airport restaurants** (up to a cap)
- **₹1,000 of free shopping** at airport retail
- **Free airport spa**

For someone who travels even once a month, ₹20,000 is trivial to hit, and the perks are worth far more than that. I have personally redeemed:

- Free shopping at **Miniso (Bangalore airport)** — bought a soft toy for my sister
- Free **Starbucks coffee** at Delhi airport on an early-morning flight
- Free **Bath & Body Works** body wash worth ₹1,000

I have not redeemed the free spa or the unlimited lounge yet — those are still on my list.

#### 4. Visa network = accepted everywhere

Both Niyo and Scapia use Visa (in my case), so this is technically a tie. But it's worth calling out because I also carry an **HDFC Diners Black Metal** card, and Diners has been declined at multiple places — including in **Bali and Dubai**. Scapia on the Visa network saved me there, especially on big-ticket purchases.

#### 5. The forex rate is genuinely good

The Scapia app shows me a running tally of "forex savings" — for my trips this past year that number is **₹4,750** based on FX alone. That doesn't include the coin rewards or the airport redemptions.

## The two trip stories that sold me on Scapia

### Story 1: The iPhone 17 Pro in Japan

We were on a Japan trip in November 2025. iPhones in Japan are infamously cheaper than in India once you account for tax. The iPhone 17 Pro I wanted was around **₹1,00,000 in Japan**.

Two things made this possible with Scapia and not Niyo:

1. **The ₹5,00,000 credit limit** absorbed the purchase in a single swipe. With a deposit-based card, I would have had to pre-fund the entire amount before the trip — and travel with a lot less wiggle room for other expenses.
2. **0% forex markup** meant the Japanese yen price translated directly into rupees, with no 2-4% bank markup eating into the savings.

End result: I got the latest iPhone roughly **₹35,000 cheaper** than the India retail price. With Niyo this purchase would have been theoretically possible — but only if I had been able to lock up ₹1L+ of cash in their wallet before leaving.

### Story 2: The Bali ATM that beat Bangalore's offline exchange

Bali is notorious for terrible cash exchange rates. We carried a stack of cash from India and burned through it fast — the rates at local shops were brutal.

Out of options, I tried a **Scapia ATM withdrawal in Bali**.

The total cost of that withdrawal — including ATM fees and FX — came out **cheaper per rupee than what I had paid in Bangalore at Orient Exchange** before the trip. That genuinely shocked me. I had assumed cash from India was always the safer bet.

The lesson I took away: for any country where local cash exchange is sketchy, withdrawing at the ATM with a 0%-forex card is almost always the better play — even after ATM fees.

## Where I'd still pick Niyo (or another product)

I want to be fair. Niyo is not a bad card. There are real situations where it might still be the right pick:

- **You can't or don't want a credit card.** If your CIBIL is thin, or you specifically want a prepaid product to control spending, Niyo is fine.
- **You don't trust yourself with credit limits.** A prepaid forex card is, by design, harder to overspend on.
- **You want a debit product for ATM-heavy trips.** Some travellers prefer a debit instrument for cash withdrawals to avoid cash-advance interest. Scapia treats foreign ATM withdrawals as a regular transaction in my experience, but always check current terms.

## Hidden costs you should still check

A few honest caveats so you go in with eyes open:

- **Replacement card fee.** I lost my Scapia card once and paid roughly **₹250** for a replacement. Not a big deal, but worth knowing.
- **Foreign ATM fees vary.** Some foreign banks charge their own ATM fee that has nothing to do with Scapia. Always check the on-screen fee before confirming a withdrawal.
- **Big-ticket purchases hit your CIBIL utilization.** A ₹1L swipe against a ₹5L limit is fine — but if you maxed it out, your credit utilization for that month would spike. Pay the bill in full to avoid CIBIL drag.
- **Reward coin value is set by Scapia.** They could change the redemption rate any time. Treat coins as a nice-to-have, not the primary reason to get the card.

## FAQ

**Is Scapia really a zero forex credit card?**
Yes. There is no forex markup on international transactions. The only foreign-currency cost I've seen is third-party ATM fees, which are charged by the ATM operator, not Scapia.

**Do I need a Federal Bank account to get the Scapia card?**
No. The card is issued by Federal Bank as the partner, but you do not need to open a separate Federal Bank savings account. The application is end-to-end inside the Scapia app.

**Is there an annual fee on the Scapia card?**
My card has been lifetime free. Always confirm the latest fee structure on Scapia's app or website before applying — these terms can change.

**Can I use Scapia for UPI?**
Yes. And — uniquely for a credit card — you earn Scapia Coins on UPI spends too. This is a real edge over most other credit cards.

**What is the airport benefit threshold?**
Spend **₹20,000 in a calendar month** to unlock airport privileges (lounge, meals, ₹1,000 shopping, spa) for the following month. I have hit this in every recent month without trying.

**Is Niyo Global still worth it in 2026?**
It depends on what variant they offer now. The deposit-based version I used in 2024 has been superseded. If you're not credit-card-eligible, it's still a fine zero-forex product — but for anyone who is eligible for Scapia, Scapia gives more for the same forex saving.
